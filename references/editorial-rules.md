# Editorial Rules

## Goal

Produce briefs for an AI content creator, not a general news reader.

This means the output should answer:

- What happened?
- Why does it matter for creators, builders, or operators?
- Is there a usable workflow, prompt, tool, or angle here?
- Is this worth turning into content?

## Keep

Prioritize items that have one or more of these traits:

- first-hand launch or feature update from an official or builder source
- concrete workflow with reproducible steps
- prompt, template, demo, repo, or tool link
- viral AIGC output with a plausible method behind it
- a product or model change that affects creator behavior
- a cross-border angle worth translating between Chinese and global audiences

## Drop Or Downrank

Avoid or heavily downrank:

- generic hype without substance
- reposts that add no new information
- simple reactions to already-known launches
- personal updates with no creator or AI relevance
- engagement bait, giveaways, and vanity milestones

## Preferred Framing

When writing summaries:

- be concrete
- avoid inflated language
- name the product, workflow, or signal explicitly
- separate facts from inference

## Suggested Creator Angles

Useful creator angles include:

- short thread or post idea
- demo breakdown
- workflow reverse engineering
- China versus overseas perspective gap
- builder case study
- what this launch changes in practice

## Escalation Rule

If an item looks promising but the post is too shallow, prefer:

1. opening the linked article, repo, or launch page
2. extracting the core fact set
3. writing the brief from the primary source

Do not overstate confidence when the post itself is weak.
